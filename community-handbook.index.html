Section 01 — Handbook

Introduction

🎯 Mission Statement

The Community Department of CUSA is dedicated to fostering a positive, engaging, and safe environment for all members of Clockwork's United State of America. We serve as the frontline representatives of our community, ensuring that every interaction reflects the values and standards of our organization.

📋 Purpose of This Handbook

This handbook has been compiled to provide all Community Department staff with clear, comprehensive guidelines regarding their roles, responsibilities, and operational procedures. It is a living document that may be updated as our department evolves.

  • Establish clear expectations for all ranks
  • Provide standardized procedures for events and moderation
  • Define the chain of command and reporting structure
  • Ensure accountability through documented quotas and logs

🏛️ About the Department

The Community Department operates under the Tedder Management Team and is responsible for community engagement, moderation, event coordination, and staff development. Our team is structured into three command levels to ensure efficient operation and clear lines of authority.

All personnel are expected to read, understand, and acknowledge this handbook before commencing their duties.

Section 02 — Operations

Responsibilities

⚖️ General Conduct

As a representative of the CUSA Community Department, your conduct both on and off duty reflects upon the entire organization. The following standards must be upheld at all times:

  • Professionalism: Maintain a respectful and professional demeanor in all community interactions
  • Impartiality: Treat all community members fairly and without bias
  • Confidentiality: Do not disclose internal discussions, logs, or sensitive information
  • Activity: Maintain consistent activity levels as defined by your rank's quota
  • Chain of Command: Always report through the proper channels

🛡️ Core Duties

All Community Department staff share the following core responsibilities:

  • Moderation: Monitor community spaces and enforce rules consistently
  • Support: Assist community members with inquiries and concerns
  • Documentation: Maintain accurate logs of all moderation actions and tickets
  • Events: Participate in and help coordinate community events
  • Training: Attend Officer Training sessions and development programs

📏 Disciplinary Standards

Failure to meet responsibilities may result in disciplinary action. The severity of action depends on the nature and frequency of the violation:

  • Verbal Warning: Minor infractions or first-time offenses
  • Written Warning: Repeated minor infractions or moderate offenses
  • Suspension: Serious violations or failure to meet quota consistently
  • Termination: Severe misconduct or gross negligence
Section 03 — Operations

Events & Giveaways

🎁 Event Logging Procedures

All events and giveaways must be properly logged to ensure transparency, track participation, and maintain records for departmental review. Proper logging is mandatory and contributes to your weekly quota.

Failure to log events properly may result in the event not counting toward your quota and potential disciplinary review.

📝 How to Log Events

Follow these steps to ensure your events are properly recorded:

  • Step 1: Access the official logging platform designated by High Command
  • Step 2: Fill out all required fields including event type, date, time, and participants
  • Step 3: Attach any relevant screenshots or evidence of the event
  • Step 4: Submit the log and await approval from a superior officer
  • Step 5: Retain a copy of your log ID for your records

🎮 Giveaway Guidelines

When conducting giveaways, adhere to the following protocols:

  • All giveaways must be pre-approved by Middle or High Command
  • Use only approved giveaway tools and methods
  • Document winner selection process for fairness verification
  • Log giveaway details within 24 hours of completion
  • Ensure prizes are distributed promptly and documented

📊 Event Types

The following event categories are recognized for quota and logging purposes:

  • Community Events: Game nights, tournaments, social gatherings
  • Training Events: Officer Training sessions, workshops
  • Promotional Events: Recruitment drives, partnership events
  • Giveaways: Prize distributions, contests, raffles
Section 04 — Requirements

Weekly Departmental Quota

📊 Quota Overview

All Community Department personnel are required to meet weekly quotas to maintain active status. Quotas are reviewed every Sunday at 23:59 EST. Failure to meet quota for two consecutive weeks will trigger a performance review.

Logs must be submitted through the proper channels and approved by the designated authority to count toward your quota.

📖 Log Definitions

  • Moderation Logs: Documented actions taken against rule violations (warns, kicks, bans)
  • Ticket Logs: Recorded support tickets handled and resolved
  • Event Logs: Documented community events hosted or co-hosted
  • OT Logs (Officer Training): Training sessions conducted or attended
  • Minutes: Total active time (in minutes) within community spaces
  • Rank Logs (CSEC): Promotions, demotions, or rank changes processed
Section 05 — Structure

Chain of Command

⛓️ Department Hierarchy

The Community Department operates under a strict chain of command to ensure efficient decision-making and clear accountability. Always report to your immediate superior unless circumstances require escalation.

Community Director
Highest authority in the department. Oversees all operations, sets policy, and has final decision-making power.
Contact: Last Resort
Community Secretary
Middle management. Handles day-to-day operations, rank logs, event approvals, and supervises Lower Command.
Contact: For operational issues
Senior Community Moderator
Experienced moderation staff. Assists with training, handles complex tickets, and mentors CMODs.
Contact: For moderation guidance
Community Moderator
Entry-level position. Responsible for basic moderation, ticket handling, and event support.
Contact: First point of contact

📞 Contact Protocol

  • General Inquiries: Contact your immediate superior (SCMOD for CMODs, CSEC for SCMODs)
  • Urgent Issues: Escalate to the next available superior in the chain
  • Serious Violations: Report directly to Community Director
  • Administrative Requests: Submit through CSEC for processing

Jumping the chain of command without valid reason is considered a breach of protocol and may result in disciplinary action.
